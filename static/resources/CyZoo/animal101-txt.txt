It was drastically reduced in numbers by hunting, and now occupies small, protected areas that are a tiny fraction of their former ranges in North America and Europe.

It has a broad, heavy head that is carried low and cannot be raised to shoulder level.  It has a pronounced hump at the shoulders.

It prefers grass and herbs, but it will also eat twigs and leaves.

Its coarse, shaggy fur is dark brown in color. The fur grows especially long on the head, neck, and shoulders, and usually forms a beard on the chin.
