It is found in eastern and southern Africa and in tropical Asia. Most of them are solitary inhabitants of open grassland, savanna, scrub forest, or marsh, but some are now found only in deep forests.

Succulent plants, such as euphorbia, assume great importance for it, and where these plants are abundant, the animal appears to be able to survive without free water.

It is noted for its thick skin, which forms platelike folds. It is nearly or completely hairless.

It is characterized by the possession of one or two horns on the upper surface of the snout.
