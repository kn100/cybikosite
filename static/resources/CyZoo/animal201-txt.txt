It belongs to one of several genera of relatively small, short-necked, large-billed waterfowl, and inhabits many areas of the world.

It eats seeds, grass and small water dwelling bugs.

It is distinguished from the other waterfowl, in that a male has sheds twice annually, the female lays large batches of smooth-shelled eggs, both sexes have overlapping scales on the skin of the leg, and exhibit some degree of sexual differentiation in plumage and in call.

There are three major groups of this bird: dabbling, diving, and perching, each based on their characteristic behaviors.
