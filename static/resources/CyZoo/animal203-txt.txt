It breeds in large colonies on the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of tropical and subtropical America, in Africa, and southern Europe and Asia.

When it eats, it walks in shallow water, stirring up living creatures, especially minute mollusks and crustaceans.  It strains these from the muddy water by means of its sievelike, thin bill.

It has webbed feet, a slender body, a long, thin neck, large wings, and a short tail.

Its plumage is mainly pink, tinged with light vermilion. It has a bare face.
