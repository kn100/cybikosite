This bird is restricted in distribution to the New World, where the greatest variety and number of species occur in South America. About 12 species are found regularly in the United States and Canada.

Its bill, which is adapted for drawing nectar from certain types of flowers, is usually rather long and always slender.

It's usually placed with the swallows, but sometimes separated in their own order. It's often called paradise bird.

It is a very small bird, and many of them are minute. It has a brilliant, glittering coloration and elaborately painted feathers.
