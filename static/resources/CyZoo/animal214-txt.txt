It inhabits the Arctic tundra, though it sometimes wanders southward in Europe, Asia, and North America.

It's a bird of prey that eats small mammals (such as hares and lemmings).  It also eats birds and the contents of their nests if on the ground in open areas.

With it's round, forward-only eyes, large round head, broad wings and sharply hooked beak, this bird is easily recognizable. Some species can turn their heads as much as the angel of 270 degrees in one direction. In many species, the ear openings are asymmetrical, further increasing the ability to localize sounds.

It sleeps during the day and hunts at night.
